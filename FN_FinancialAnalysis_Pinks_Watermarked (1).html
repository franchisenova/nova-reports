⚑ RED FLAG — Affiliate NOI of 47.8% is Structurally Misleading

The affiliate-owned Austin location reports 47.8% NOI ($358K on $748K revenue). This figure excludes royalty (7%), brand fund (2%), local advertising management ($500/mo + 3% of gross), and tech fees ($650–$1,000/mo) — all fees that franchisees are required to pay. Adjusting for these fees, a franchisee replicating the same $748K revenue would have an effective NOI of approximately 17–20%, or ~$127–$150K. That is still a strong return IF the affiliate's revenue is replicable — but there is zero franchisee data to confirm it is. The 47.8% figure should not be used as a benchmark for franchise economics under any circumstances.

Green Flags & Red Flags
✓ Green Flags
  • Affiliate-owned location showing strong AUV ($748K) in home market
  • CEO Model structure is explicitly designed for semi-absentee multi-unit operators — well-designed ownership architecture
  • Zero terminations, closures, or non-renewals to date
  • Low single-unit entry cost ($128K–$167K) vs comparable systems
  • 10% veteran/first-responder discount available
  • Transfer fee of 20% of initial fee is relatively standard for the category
  • 10-year initial term with up to 4 successive 5-year renewals provides long-term security if the system succeeds
⚑ Red Flags
  • Zero franchisee financial performance data disclosed — the single most critical red flag. This is not disclosed and IS a red flag.
  • Franchisor formed May 2023 — 12 months of operating history as a franchise system. Insufficient track record to evaluate viability.
  • Franchisor is pre-profitability — net loss of $29K. Revenue dependent on initial franchise fees, not royalties.
  • No exclusive territory granted — "You will not receive an exclusive territory." Fundamental structural risk for multi-unit operators.
  • Total fee burden ~12.5–14% is above the industry amber threshold of 12%.
  • Transactional revenue only — no subscription or recurring model. Revenue is fully weather/season dependent.
  • No SBA preferred lenders identified — financing path is unclear.
  • Affiliate NOI of 47.8% is structurally misleading — does not reflect franchisee fee obligations.
