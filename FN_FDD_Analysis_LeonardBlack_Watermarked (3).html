FN Recommendation
Scenthound — Proceed to Discovery Day

Scenthound is the right system for Leonard at this stage. The recommendation is grounded directly in his own investment thesis. Leonard stated he looks for recurring subscription revenue, shared labor deployment across units, and moderate asset intensity. Scenthound delivers all three: a $40–$60/month membership model with industry-leading retention, a GM-layer structure that scales across Scenters with shared back-office, and moderate build-out costs without heavy equipment or real estate requirements. The 4-unit development agreement reduces per-territory fees by 40% — making his stated target of 4+ units the optimal entry structure, not just a preference. Zero terminations across 3 years of rapid growth is the strongest system health signal in this comparison by a significant margin.

Pink's Window Services is not recommended for Leonard at this time for three specific reasons. First, it is a transactional revenue model — no subscription, no recurring membership — which directly contradicts the thesis Leonard articulated on his intake form. Second, the franchisor was formed in May 2023 with no independent franchisee financial performance data available; Leonard would be investing in a system with a single affiliate-owned location as the only benchmark. Third, the territory is not exclusive — in a multi-unit model, the absence of protected territory creates a structural risk to Leonard's entire investment. These are not solvable through capital structuring. They are fundamental mismatches between the system's architecture and Leonard's stated criteria.

What FN Can Unlock for Leonard
  • SBA preferred lender introductions who work with net-worth-backed candidates — Leonard's $1M+ net worth is the primary capital lever, not liquid cash, and FN lenders understand how to structure around that
  • Franchisor introduction to Scenthound's development team for territory confirmation in Leonard's target markets and multi-unit agreement negotiation for 4+ territories
  • Capital stack modeling for the 4-unit development path — including equity extraction options from Leonard's net worth to meet the liquid capital requirement
Risks to Watch — Scenthound
  • Liquid capital gap is the primary constraint: Leonard's $50K–$100K liquid is well below the $549K planning amount. Net worth of $1M+ provides options (equity extraction, HELOC, cash-out refi) but these require documentation and timeline. This is FN's core work for this deal.
  • Bottom-quartile NOI spread (1.2% vs 13.8% median): The spread between top and bottom quartile is wide. Understand what drives underperformance — specifically ask whether labor management and GM quality is the primary differentiator, since that is exactly what Leonard will be relying on.
  • Semi-absentee narrative for SBA lender: Any SBA lender will require a written GM candidate, Scenthound corporate support documentation, and a management continuity plan. This needs to be structured before lender submission.
  • Territory availability in target markets: Cannot be confirmed from the FDD — must be verified directly with Scenthound's development team. If Leonard's target geography is unavailable, the entire analysis needs to be revisited with a new system.
  • Renewal terms (Item 23): Renewal is at franchisor discretion with conditions including upgrades, additional training, and a general release. Confirm the specific renewal conditions during Discovery Day before executing the franchise agreement.
