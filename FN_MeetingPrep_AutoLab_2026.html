Who You're Meeting
MS

Mark Sullivan

Co-Owner & Director of Operations — Auto-Lab Complete Car Care Centers

Mark Sullivan was Auto-Lab's top-performing franchisee before being brought in as co-owner and partner by CEO Stephen Wilson in 2023. He runs the Plymouth, Belleville, and Brighton Michigan locations personally. His stated mission is to create "carbon copies" of himself in different states — meaning he views the area operator model as a way to replicate his own operational playbook nationally. He personally developed the new area operator model that is now Auto-Lab's primary expansion vehicle. He is the public face of Auto-Lab's growth story and was quoted extensively in Crain's Detroit Business June 2025 feature on the company's national expansion plans.

FN Alignment Analysis — Where We Connect & Where We Don't

Where FN & Auto-Lab Align

  • Auto-Lab is in the Automotive vertical — one of FN's 10 core sectors (non-discretionary, recession-resistant)
  • Mark Sullivan is actively seeking franchisees to scale nationally — FN's pipeline could be a direct source of qualified candidates
  • Their area operator model ($75K per quadrant) is an accessible entry for the right FN candidate — lower capital than a full unit
  • SBA approved — aligns with FN's capital structuring approach for candidates
  • Multi-unit mindset — Sullivan's entire growth model is built on scale, which matches FN's mission

Where There's Tension

  • Auto-Lab is not a recurring revenue model — automotive repair is primarily transactional (per-visit, per-repair). FN's mission centers on membership/subscription recurring revenue
  • FPI health score of 48/100 is below average — thin SBA lending footprint, franchisee turnover worth watching
  • No Item 19 in the FDD — candidates cannot see validated unit-level financials without asking directly
  • The Wanda & Larry situation — a prior FN-disqualified candidate is close with Mark. This dynamic needs to be navigated carefully

What FN Can Offer Auto-Lab

  • A qualified pipeline of pre-screened candidates specifically in the Automotive vertical
  • FN's capital structuring expertise — helping candidates get SBA-ready before they approach Auto-Lab
  • Chipo and the team can serve as an advisory layer between Auto-Lab and prospective franchisees — vetting before referral
  • Texas territory opportunity is currently open and FN has Dallas-area candidates in the pipeline (or will)

Watch Points Going Into the Meeting

  • The Wanda & Larry dynamic — Robin disqualified them on financials, yet they remain close with Mark. Don't volunteer this history; let Mark bring it up if he does
  • FN's recurring revenue thesis doesn't naturally fit Auto-Lab's transactional model — be clear about FN's candidate profile rather than overpromising fit
  • Auto-Lab is in active expansion mode — they may want to move fast. Protect FN's vetting process and don't let urgency override due diligence
  • The 11% franchisee turnover in 2023 and thin FDD disclosures are worth noting internally — don't commit to referring candidates without reviewing the current FDD first
Suggested Questions for the Meeting
  • Walk us through the area operator model in detail — what does day-to-day operations look like for an area operator versus a single-unit franchisee, and what does success look like in year one?
  • You mentioned territory expansion in Florida, Texas, and Arizona — what does the ideal franchisee profile look like for those markets, and what have you seen work in terms of background and capital?
  • How does Auto-Lab support franchisees who come from outside the automotive industry? What's the learning curve been like for non-car-background operators?
  • The area operator quadrant model is new — how has the Florida pilot performed so far, and what adjustments have you made?
  • How do you see organizations like Franchise Nova fitting into your growth pipeline — are you looking for franchise advisor partnerships, or more of a referral relationship?
  • What does the Auto-Lab Express quick-service concept look like — is that a separate franchise offering or a format variation within the existing system?
Context — The Wanda & Larry Situation
⚠️
What we know: Wanda and Larry are past Auto-Lab franchisees who were introduced to FN at some point. Robin (FN's advisor) disqualified them — the decision was made after their financials showed no promise. They remain personally close with Mark Sullivan.
⚠️
Why it matters: Mark may bring them up, reference them positively, or use the relationship as context for the FN conversation. There may be an expectation or assumption that FN should work with them again or reconsider.
🚩
How to handle it: Do not volunteer the history. If it comes up, acknowledge the relationship warmly without relitigating the disqualification decision. FN's vetting process is a feature, not a flaw — the goal is to protect all parties including the franchisor from undercapitalized candidates.
✓
The frame to use if needed: "FN's process is designed to make sure every candidate we refer is genuinely ready — financially and operationally. That protects you as much as it protects them."
